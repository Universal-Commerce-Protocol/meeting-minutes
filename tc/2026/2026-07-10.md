## 2. General Topics

- **Topic:** Governance Enforcement Update
 - **Notes:** Federico D'Amato announced that a new governance system for repositories was successfully rolled out earlier in the week, replacing the static code owners files for all but the main repository. This system is expected to extend to the main repository by next week. This change introduces a secondary "sanity check" for cross-vertical dependencies, ensuring that ground-level approval processes remain consistent and secure.
- **Topic:** Payment Terms and Deferred Payments
 - **Notes:** Daniel Wyckoff explained the focus was on deferred payments at checkout, while Drew Olson noted that the protocol might not be the primary mechanism for initiating future charges. The group discussed the need for clear, concrete use cases, such as lodging, to guide the design, and Daniel Wyckoff agreed to work on an RFC to outline these requirements.
- **Topic:** Review of Outstanding PRs for v8
 - **Notes:** Demian Raspall requested that the team prioritize reviews for several outstanding PRs (see Action Items for details).

## 2. Technical Decisions & Consensus

- **Topic:** Feeds Domain Working Group Formation
 - **Discussion:** James Andersen followed up on the proposal to form a Feeds Domain Working Group to address complex feed-related and bulk product discovery issues. Members emphasized the importance of involving domain experts from the broader ecosystem to refine these standards. Uddhav Kambli inquired about the scope of the feed-related work, specifically questioning whether it extends to search engine optimization (SEO), geospatial data (GEO), or social commerce beyond agentic commerce. James Andersen and Amit Handa confirmed that scoping these requirements is a primary objective for the newly proposed Catalog Domain Working Group, as current feed implementations are highly complex.
 - **Agreement:** Agreed to start working on the process to create new Domain Working Groups which can be used for creating DWG for Feeds.
 - **Context:** [RFC #550 Bulk product discovery](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/issues/550)
- **Topic:** V8 Release Roadmap Scope
 - **Discussion:** The council reviewed the proposed list of critical capabilities and priorities targeted for the upcoming V8 release, taking into account the significant workload constraints and upcoming holiday vacations over the next 3–4 weeks.
 - **Agreement:** Agreed on the proposed list of critical capabilities and priorities to be targeted for the upcoming V8 release.
 - **Context:** [v8 Roadmap](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/milestone/3)

## 2. Open Discussions & TC PR Reviews

- **Topic:** Product Additional Fields (PR #481)
 - **Notes:** Ilya Grigorik and James Andersen discussed a PR regarding additional product fields, which would provide necessary customization for agentic commerce, such as legal disclosures or product-specific options.
 - **Context:** [feat(shopping): Add additional fields proposal (from Shopify)](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/issues/481)
- **Topic:** Actions Negotiation and Discovery (PR #553)
 - **Notes:** Ilya Grigorik and Amit Handa discussed if actions require discovery, arguing that this approach is counterintuitive and that actions should not bypass the negotiation process. TC decided to pause the PR and schedule a dedicated discussion to explore an alternative design based on common message and signal patterns.
 - **Context:** [feat: Introduce Actions and apply it to Checkout](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/issues/553)
- **Topic:** Constraints PR (PR #424)
 - **Notes:** James Andersen and Daniel Wyckoff discussed a PR regarding constraints, aimed at resolving tech debt related to tokenization (specifically CVV requirements). Daniel Wyckoff indicated that the PR was close to completion, and the group agreed to review the latest updates to see if it is ready for release.
 - **Context:** [feat: structured constraints for instruments and beyond](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/issues/424)
