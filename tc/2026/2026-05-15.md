# UCP Tech Council (TC) Meeting - 2026-05-15

**Led by:** Amit Handa

## Attendance

- **Present:** Imran Hoosain, Maxime Najim, Naga Malepati, Drew Olson, Ilya Grigorik, Lee Richmond, Anurag Sinha, Amit Handa, Ryan Cheung, Demian Raspall, James Andersen, Scot DeDeo, Greg Smith, Patrick Jordan, Jing Li


## 1. Technical Discussions & Decisions

- **Topic:** Meeting Efficiency & Structure
  - **Discussion:** Discussed guidelines to keep meetings high-bandwidth and focused, specifically avoiding extensive PR discussions in the main forum.
  - **Decision:** Approved limiting pull request discussions to a maximum of 5–7 minutes per item to prevent deep dives.

- **Topic:** Operational Parallelization
  - **Discussion:** Evaluated moving from sequential PR reviews to parallelized task processing to increase execution speed.
  - **Decision:** Approved adopting a parallelized review strategy for PR milestones, using core teams of 2–3 owners to conduct initial deep dives before presenting to the council.

- **Topic:** Standardized Backporting and Bug Fix Announcement
  - **Discussion:** Discussed backporting non-breaking changes and critical bug fixes to the recently launched April 8th version.
  - **Decision:** Approved bundling critical breaking changes (such as the schema fix and identity linking foundation) and bug fixes into a single community announcement and patch.

- **Topic:** Namespace Uniqueness Enforcement
  - **Discussion:** Discussed potential namespace overlaps between common and vertical directories.
  - **Decision:** Approved build-time enforcement of namespace uniqueness between common and vertical types to prevent accidental collisions.

- **Topic:** Third-Party Capability Hosting
  - **Notes:** Amit Handa raised design concerns regarding scenarios where third-party providers host specific capabilities (e.g., checkout or orders) on behalf of a merchant. This introduces complexity around identity and cross-service verification. Ilya Grigorik noted that while delegation is sensible, it requires a secure mechanism for identity and regulatory proof. James Andersen added that different marketplaces operate under varying regional models, making this an important open topic for future discussion.

- **Topic:** Inline Profiles for Personal Agents
  - **Notes:** Ilya Grigorik proposed allowing "inline profiles" for agents to lower the testing and development barrier, which currently requires hosting a public profile. Amit Handa agreed to add this topic to the icebox for future consideration.
